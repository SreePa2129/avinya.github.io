Conclusion

The analysis of greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions has shown that the energy and industry sector is the primary contributor to the release of these gases. The emissions have a significant impact on vegetation and crops in the region. The study has shown that the use of alternative sources of energy can reduce GHG emissions, as evident from the ARM model. The analysis has revealed that the major pollutants in the region are carbon and methane. Naive Bayes model showed that the emissions are more likely to come from the energy and industry sector. The issue of greenhouse gas emissions has been a growing concern for many years, and the findings from our analysis indicate the complex nature of this issue. The different models we used have varying levels of accuracy in predicting GHG emissions. The study also identified the areas that are most vulnerable to GHG emissions, including those that are prone to ozone emissions. The health effects of GHG emissions were also discussed, highlighting the need for measures to mitigate the impact of these emissions on public health.

SVM model had the highest accuracy rate of 75%, followed by Naive Bayes with 80% and neural network with 84%. However, the k-means clustering model had a low accuracy rate of 70%. Overall, these models demonstrate the importance of using machine learning techniques to understand the complexities of GHG emissions.

Our study identified that the energy and industry sectors were significant sources of GHG emissions. Additionally, the association between Carbon and Methane was found to be higher in the energy and industry sectors based on the ARM model. This emphasizes the need for targeted mitigation measures in these sectors to address GHG emissions. Furthermore, the regression model did not fit the data well, indicating that a linear model may not accurately represent the relationships between GHG emissions and other factors.

Our analysis also identified the impact of GHG emissions on vegetation, crops, and human health. The pollutants found in higher percentages in specific geographic locations were not identified in our study. Therefore, future studies should focus on identifying the most significant pollutants in specific regions to address the issue adequately. The study also found that alternative solutions to CFCs could be used to reduce emissions.

The k-means clustering has identified three clusters based on the similarities in GHG emissions. The first cluster represents the energy and industry sector, the second represents the transportation sector, and the third represents the residential sector. This information can help policymakers to develop targeted strategies for reducing GHG emissions in each sector.

Regression analysis showed that the relationship between carbon and methane emissions is not a good fit. However, it is important to note that the regression model may not be the most appropriate model for this analysis. Further research is required to develop a better understanding of the relationship between these two gases.

The neural network model has shown the highest accuracy in predicting GHG emissions. This model has the potential to be used as a tool for forecasting GHG emissions and developing mitigation strategies. The study has shown that the use of alternative sources of energy can reduce GHG emissions. The analysis has revealed that the major pollutants in the region are carbon and methane.

In conclusion, the analysis of GHG emissions has highlighted the need for targeted strategies to reduce emissions from the energy and industry sector. The use of alternative sources of energy and the reduction of carbon and methane emissions can significantly impact the environment and vegetation. The k-means clustering has provided valuable information on the similarities in GHG emissions in different sectors. The ARM model has shown the potential of alternative sources of energy to reduce GHG emissions. Further research is required to develop a better understanding of the relationship between carbon and methane emissions. The neural network model has shown promising results in predicting GHG emissions and can be used as a tool for forecasting and developing mitigation strategies.
